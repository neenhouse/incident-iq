# IncidentIQ -- Vision

## North Star

Transform incident management from reactive firefighting into proactive intelligence. IncidentIQ turns your incident history into actionable patterns, predictions, and automated response playbooks -- enabling engineering teams to prevent incidents before they happen and resolve them faster when they do.

## Problem Statement

Engineering teams accumulate years of incident data across PagerDuty, Opsgenie, Jira, and Slack, but rarely extract systematic intelligence from it. The result:

1. **Repeated incidents** -- The same root causes trigger incidents month after month because patterns go undetected.
2. **Slow response** -- On-call engineers reinvent troubleshooting steps because institutional knowledge lives in people's heads, not runbooks.
3. **No predictive capability** -- Teams cannot anticipate which systems are approaching failure or which changes carry outsized risk.
4. **Blame-oriented culture** -- Without measurement, organizations default to finger-pointing instead of systemic improvement.
5. **Opaque metrics** -- MTTR and MTTA are tracked superficially if at all, hiding trends that could drive investment decisions.

## Design Principles

### 1. Data-first, opinion-second
Import raw incident data from any source. Let the patterns emerge from evidence rather than imposing frameworks prematurely.

### 2. Actionable over informational
Every insight must connect to a concrete next step: a runbook to create, a system to harden, a process to change, a prediction to monitor.

### 3. Blameless by design
Surface systemic factors, not individual mistakes. Score and celebrate blameless culture practices. Make the human side of incident management visible and improvable.

### 4. Progressive disclosure
Start with a clear dashboard summary. Let users drill into patterns, predictions, and recommendations at their own pace. Never overwhelm.

### 5. Time-aware intelligence
Incidents exist in time. Trends matter more than snapshots. Show how metrics evolve, when patterns cluster, and where the trajectory leads.
